# Forefront Golf Daily and Progressive Practice Prescription

**Purpose:** Turn the Forefront Golf Practice Session Builder research into practical daily, weekly, and block-level recommendations for changing a golfer. This document is designed to become the “dosage and progression” layer behind the Practice Session Builder.

**Status:** Pre-production coaching logic. Use as the working prescription standard, but keep thresholds tagged `review_required` until Brendan validates with real clients and TrackMan/3D data.

---

## Executive Prescription

The practical rule is simple: **one primary change, one proof metric, one progression lane, one retest**. Motor learning research in golf supports structured practice design that progresses from blocked acquisition toward variable, random, and representative practice rather than staying in repetitive blocked practice ([Barzyk & Gruber 2024 systematic review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10899359/)). External-focus cueing should be the default client-facing language because external focus generally improves motor learning compared with internal body-part focus, while internal/biomechanical language stays in the coach explanation layer ([Wulf & Su 2007 / review context](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1469029223001875)).

Forefront’s practice dose should be organized around **learning blocks**, not just total balls hit. A “good” session contains a readiness/warmup gate, a proof test, a focused acquisition or skill block, a variability/transfer bridge when stage-appropriate, a retest, and a closing homework/no-contamination instruction. Sleep and post-session interference matter because motor learning consolidation is sleep-sensitive, so new swing-change sessions should avoid competing motor patterns later that day when possible ([Walker 2005](https://walkerlab.berkeley.edu/reprints/Walker_ClinSportsMed_05.pdf)).

## Forefront Dosage Units

### Core units

| Unit | Definition | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **RLU** | Rep Learning Unit: one intentional rep with a clear cue, target, and feedback condition. Can be no-ball, slow-ball, or full shot. | Controls acquisition volume better than “balls hit.” |
| **Shot cluster** | 5–10 shots under the same scoring condition. | Proof test, baseline, retest. |
| **Set** | 3–6 RLUs grouped under one cue/constraint. | Acquisition and serial blocks. |
| **Transfer rep** | One rep with full routine, target, decision, and outcome scoring. | Random, representative, pressure, course blocks. |
| **High-intent rep** | Speed, max-effort, or aggressive force/power attempt. | Speed/GRF work; requires green readiness. |

### Daily caps

| Client state | New-pattern acquisition cap | High-intent cap | Pressure cap | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| **Healthy adult, coached** | 12–18 RLUs/day | 18–36 high-intent reps/session if speed-specific | 5–20 pressure reps if consolidation+ | `review_required` but aligned with Forefront AMG cap. |
| **Solo practice** | 8–12 RLUs/day | Avoid unless already trained | 0–10 reps only if stable | Solo should reinforce, not introduce. |
| **Junior** | 6–10 RLUs/day | Low volume, game-based | Game pressure only | Shorter attention window. |
| **Senior / mobility-limited** | 8–12 RLUs/day | Low or none until warmup gate passes | Low | Extend warmup; reduce fatigue. |
| **Pain 3–4/10** | 0–8 modified RLUs | None | None | Reduce volume 30–50%; no new high-load pattern. |
| **Pain ≥5/10 or red flag** | 0 | 0 | 0 | Stop, modify, or refer. Pain-monitor approaches support load modification and referral decisions in rehab settings ([Silbernagel 2007](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17307888/)). |
| **Poor sleep <6 hr** | 0–8 maintenance RLUs | None | None | Avoid new acquisition/speed. |
| **Tournament within 3 days** | 0 new-pattern RLUs | None or very low | Routine/feel only | Confidence and transfer, not rebuild. |

## Session Duration Prescriptions

### 20-minute quick practice

| Block | Minutes | Volume | Drill type | Scoring |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Arrival/intention | 1 | 1 decision | “What is the one thing?” | None |
| Warmup/activation | 3 | 6–10 movements | Mobility + pattern rehearsal | Pain/readiness check |
| Proof/baseline | 3 | 5 shots or 5 putts | Relevant test only | Record simple pass/fail |
| Main work | 8 | 12–18 RLUs | One primary drill family | 65–70% success target if beyond pure acquisition |
| Retest | 3 | 5 shots or 5 putts | Same as baseline | Compare to baseline |
| Homework | 2 | 1 assignment | Same cue only | No-contamination note |

**Use for:** maintenance, quick reinforcement, putting start-line, low-point checkpoint, TrackMan quick bay.

**Do not use for:** brand-new complex swing change, speed session, pressure/scoring progression, multi-drill session.

### 30-minute bay practice

| Block | Minutes | Volume | Drill type |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Arrival + readiness | 2 | 1 goal + gate | Pain/sleep/fatigue check |
| Warmup + pattern bridge | 5 | 10–15 movements + 5 rehearsals | Physical prep + slow rehearsal |
| Proof test | 4 | 5–8 shots | TrackMan/strike/start-line test |
| Acquisition or calibration | 12 | 18–24 RLUs | One primary drill; optional constraint |
| Retest | 4 | 5–8 shots | Same proof test |
| Homework | 3 | 1 cue + 1 volume rule | Client-facing assignment |

**Use for:** acquisition, skill calibration, equipment A/B, putting/short game micro-session.

### 45-minute practice

| Block | Minutes | Volume | Drill type |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Arrival + warmup | 8 | 10–20 movements | Prep + branch-specific activation |
| Proof test | 5 | 5–10 shots | Baseline |
| Blocked acquisition | 12 | 18–24 RLUs | Primary drill |
| Serial bridge | 8 | 12–16 RLUs | Same intent, changed target/club/lie |
| Transfer/retest | 8 | 8–12 reps | Full routine + retest |
| Reflection/homework | 4 | 1 assignment | No-contamination if new change |

**Use for:** standard coached or solo session when one goal is active.

### 60-minute standard coached session

| Block | Minutes | Volume | Drill type | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Arrival/intention | 3 | 1 goal | Session target | Tie to diagnostic branch. |
| Warmup | 8–10 | 12–20 movements | Corrective + dynamic | Extend if senior/mobility-limited. |
| Pattern rehearsal | 5 | 8–12 no-ball/slow reps | Cue rehearsal | One active cue. |
| Proof test | 6 | 5–10 shots | Branch test | Baseline before drills. |
| Blocked acquisition | 15 | 18–30 RLUs | Primary drill | Only one primary intent. |
| Serial variability | 8 | 12–18 reps | Same intent, mild variation | Change target/club/lie. |
| Retest | 6 | 5–10 shots | Same as proof | Learning verdict. |
| Reflection/homework | 4 | 1 assignment | Home dose | No-contamination if needed. |

**Optional swap:** If stage is retention/transfer, reduce blocked acquisition to 5–8 minutes and add 10–15 minutes of random/representative practice.

### 90-minute lesson + practice integration

| Block | Minutes | Volume | Drill type |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Arrival/readiness | 5 | Gate check | Pain/sleep/fatigue/round proximity |
| Full warmup | 12–15 | 20–30 movements | Physical + branch prep |
| Baseline/proof | 8 | 10 shots or full test | TrackMan/3D/video/putting/short-game |
| Technical acquisition | 20 | 24–36 RLUs | Primary + one support family max |
| Serial variability | 12 | 18–24 reps | Structured variation |
| Random/contextual | 10 | 10–15 reps | Changed club/target/lie |
| Representative transfer | 10 | 6–10 full-routine reps | PlayBox/on-course/simulator |
| Retest | 8 | Same baseline | Learning verdict |
| Homework | 5 | Daily prescription | Volume + cue + no-contamination |

## Stage-Based Progression Standard

### Global progression ladder

| Stage | Typical weeks | Primary schedule | Feedback | Tech | Drill volume | Scoring | Progress trigger |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---:|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | 1–4 | Blocked/errorless | 50–75% | High, coach-filtered | 12–18 new-pattern RLUs/day | Proof + retest only | 6/10 checkpoint or 65% clean reps. |
| **Consolidation** | 5–8 | Blocked → serial | 33–50% | Intermittent | 18–30 RLUs/session | Weekly mini-test | 65–70% success under target/club change. |
| **Retention** | 9–12 | Serial → random | 20–33% | Set-end or retest only | 20–40 varied reps | Weekly/biweekly | Pattern survives random practice. |
| **Transfer** | 13–16+ | Random → representative | 10–20% | Mostly off | 10–30 transfer reps | On-course/Combine/SG | Pattern survives routine, decision, pressure. |
| **Maintenance** | Ongoing | Spaced, random, low volume | Self-controlled | Assessment only | 20–60 min/week per skill | Monthly | Stable benchmark over 2–3 retests. |

### Daily home assignment by stage

| Stage | Daily home work | Time | Volume | Ball striking? | Goal |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Acquisition | No-ball mirror/slow-motion + 3–5 low-speed ball reps if safe | 8–12 min | 8–12 RLUs | Optional, low speed | Encode one pattern. |
| Consolidation | Rehearsal + serial ball set | 12–20 min | 12–20 RLUs | Yes | Stabilize across small variation. |
| Retention | Random target/club mini-set | 15–25 min | 15–30 reps | Yes | Self-organize without coach. |
| Transfer | Full routine, target/decision, on-course simulation | 20–45 min | 9–18 transfer reps | Yes | Take it to course context. |
| Maintenance | Spaced benchmark + feel refresh | 10–20 min, 2–3×/week | 10–20 reps | Yes | Keep pattern without dependency. |

## Goal-Specific Daily and Weekly Prescriptions

## Swing Change / Technique

### Daily recommendation

| Day type | Time | Volume | Structure | Drill families |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| **Coached acquisition day** | 45–75 min | 12–18 new-pattern RLUs + 10–20 proof/transfer reps | Warmup → proof → blocked → serial lite → retest → homework | Branch-specific: face/path, low point, GRF, sequencing, wrist. |
| **Solo reinforcement day** | 10–20 min | 8–12 RLUs | Rehearsal → 5–10 slow balls → note | Same primary drill only. |
| **No-practice consolidation day** | 5–8 min | 5–8 no-ball RLUs | Mirror/feel only | No new drills. |
| **Transfer day** | 30–60 min | 15–30 random/representative reps | Full routine, varied targets | Random club/target, PlayBox, mini-Combine. |

### 16-week progression

| Weeks | Sessions/week | Balls/reps/session | Required blocks | What to avoid | Test |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | 2 coached + 2 micro | 25–45 balls, 12–18 RLUs | Proof, blocked, retest | Pressure, random, speed, multiple cues | 5–10 shot branch proof. |
| 3–4 | 1–2 coached + 2 solo | 35–60 balls | Add serial bridge | Long sessions of blocked-only | AMG/video checkpoint. |
| 5–8 | 1 coached + 2–3 practice | 45–75 balls | Serial + some random | Constant tech display | Mini-Combine or branch retest. |
| 9–12 | 1 coached/biweekly + 2 solo | 40–70 balls | Random + representative | New pattern changes | Random target test. |
| 13–16 | 1 coached/biweekly + on-course | 20–50 balls + 9-hole transfer | Transfer + pressure | Mechanical thought during shot | DECADE/on-course audit. |

### Drill selection rule

| Branch intent | Primary drill family | Support family | Contact drill placement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Face control | Start-line, face corridor, HackMotion if wrist-derived | D-Plane education | Retest unless contact is also diagnosis. |
| Path delivery | Path gate, alignment rod, swing direction constraint | Body/sequence if proofed | Retest/transfer. |
| Strike/low point | Strike map, low-point line, tee progression | Ball-position ladder | Primary OK. |
| GRF/pressure | Recenter, lead finish, step/pump, slope, post-up | Low-speed ball proof | Contact as proof/retest. |
| Sequencing | Pump, chest-over, pause/transition, step | 3D/video feedback | Retest/serial. |
| Equipment fit | A/B equipment, strike/launch comparison | Setup confirmation | Primary OK only as proof, not technique rebuild. |

## Ground Force / Pressure / Speed Sequencing

### Daily recommendation

| Day type | Time | Volume | Structure | Drill families |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| **GRF acquisition** | 45–60 min | 12–18 GRF RLUs + 10 ball proofs | Warmup → recenter proof → blocked pressure drill → low-speed ball transfer → retest | Step, pump, lead-side finish, slope, post-up. |
| **GRF consolidation** | 45–75 min | 18–30 RLUs + 15 varied shots | Blocked → serial → random | Pump-to-full, lead-post, pressure trace, target change. |
| **GRF transfer** | 30–60 min | 15–30 transfer reps | Full routine, varied clubs, no force data during reps | PlayBox, random club/target, course sim. |

### Practical coaching rules

| Rule | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Start point | Geometry and strike first; force plate confirms, not replaces, the observable story. |
| First proof | Can the player recenter/finish lead-side in slow/no-ball and keep strike/low point acceptable? |
| Cue | “Land and post,” “push the ground away,” “finish balanced on the lead side,” not “increase vertical GRF.” |
| Tech | TrackMan 3D/video/force plate early; fade to ball flight and finish balance in transfer. |
| Progress | No-ball → slow ball → normal speed → target change → club change → course task. |
| Regression | If strike collapses, reduce speed and return to station constraint. |

### 6-week GRF progression

| Week | Frequency | Volume | Focus | Test |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| 1 | 2–3× | 12–18 RLUs/session | Recenter, lead finish, station proof | 5-shot low-speed strike + video. |
| 2 | 2–3× | 18–24 RLUs | Pump/step into ball proof | 5–10 shot strike/finish test. |
| 3–4 | 2× | 20–30 RLUs | Serial variation, target/club change | TrackMan 3D/video checkpoint. |
| 5 | 2× | 15–25 transfer reps | Random range | Dispersion + finish audit. |
| 6 | 1 coached + course | 9-hole transfer | Pattern thought on practice round only | On-course commitment/strike audit. |

## Speed / Power

### Readiness gate

Speed is allowed only when:
- pain ≤2/10,
- fatigue ≤3/10,
- sleep ≥7 hours or normal for client,
- mobility warmup passes,
- no active post-rehab restriction,
- no new swing-change acquisition conflict.

### Weekly dose

| Stage | Sessions/week | Time/session | High-intent reps | Ball-transfer reps | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Intro | 2 | 20–30 min | 12–18 | 5–8 | Learn protocol, no chasing max every rep. |
| Accumulation | 2–3 | 25–35 min | 18–36 | 5–10 | Stop before speed decay. |
| Peak/test | 2 | 20–30 min | 12–24 | 8–12 | Include driver optimizer. |
| Maintenance | 1–2 | 10–20 min | 6–18 | 3–6 | Keep speed without fatigue. |

### 8-week speed progression

| Week | Dose | Drill type | Test |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2 sessions | Baseline, movement screen, low-volume overspeed | CHS baseline. |
| 2 | 2 sessions | Level 1 speed exposure + ball-transfer | Session PR only if clean. |
| 3–5 | 3 sessions | Overspeed + med ball rotational power | Weekly CHS. |
| 6–7 | 2–3 sessions | Heel stomp / step-change / Mach 3 style expression | CHS + smash/contact. |
| 8 | 2 sessions | Deload + driver application | CHS final + optimizer. |

### Speed drill rule

| Drill type | Use when | Do not use when |
|---|---|---|
| Overspeed | Readiness green, no active acquisition conflict | Pain, fatigue, poor sleep, post-rehab, tournament week. |
| Mach 3 max effort | Speed-transfer stage, experienced player | Beginner acquisition, contact chaos, red readiness. |
| Med ball rotational | Physical training day or warmup adjunct | Back pain, poor bracing, no coaching supervision. |
| Driver optimizer | Speed exists but launch/spin poor | Smash/strike poor; equipment not checked. |

## Course Transfer / Performance

### Weekly recommendation

| Stage | Course-transfer work | Frequency | Scoring |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Acquisition | None or “pattern thought” only on practice round | 0–1× | No score pressure. |
| Consolidation | 3-hole simulation | 1×/week | Commitment + execution rating. |
| Retention | 9-hole practice round | 1×/week | Decision quality + pattern use. |
| Transfer | 9–18 hole transfer round | 1×/week | Score, SG/DECADE, penalties. |
| Maintenance | Competitive simulation | 1–2×/month | Benchmark vs personal par. |

### On-course scoring

| Metric | Protocol | Pass criterion |
|---|---|---|
| Commitment | Rate 1–5 after each shot | ≥4/5 on 70% of shots. |
| Decision quality | DECADE-style 1–5 per decision | Mean ≥3.5. |
| Pattern transfer | Did player use target focus instead of mechanics? | ≥70% of shots with target/process cue. |
| Penalty avoidance | Count penalty strokes | Downward trend over 4 rounds. |
| Recovery quality | Did player choose correct recovery? | ≥80% “smart miss” decisions. |

## What to Score and When

### Session-level scoring

| Branch | Baseline/retest | Pass target | Retest frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Face/path | 10-shot Face-to-Path or path corridor | SD <1.5–2° or improved trend | Every coached acquisition session. |
| Low point/contact | 5-shot low-point or strike map | ≥4/5 acceptable strikes | Every session until stable. |
| GRF/pressure | 5-shot finish/pressure/strike proof | ≥4/5 lead-finish + acceptable strike | Weekly or coached sessions. |
| Speed | 5 max no-ball swings + ball transfer | 3–5% gain over 4 weeks, no contact collapse | Weekly. |
| Putting start line | 10 putts through gate | ≥7/10 initially, ≥8/10 later | Weekly. |
| Putting speed | 10 putts 30 ft, 1–2 ft past | ≥8/10 | Weekly. |
| Wedge distance | 5 shots × 3 distances | ≥3/5 in window each | Weekly. |
| Bunker | 5 bunker shots | ≥4/5 exits first, then proximity | Weekly while active. |
| Course decision | 9-hole decision audit | Mean ≥3.5/5 | Monthly. |

### Season phase

| Phase | Practice emphasis | Volume |
|---|---|---|
| Off-season | Acquire and consolidate new patterns | 4–5 sessions/week. |
| Pre-season | Consolidate, test, transfer | 3–4 sessions/week. |

| Context | Allowed |
|---|---|
| Coached | New pattern, technology feedback, complex constraints, diagnosis, high-detail retest. |

| Rule | Validator |
|---|---|
